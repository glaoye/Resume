Optimising Light Paths

Show More Show Less

This was my first programming project, assigned to me in Year 1, and what ultimately sparked my passion for software development. The requirements were to produce a programme that would track the paths of multiple light rays as they traversed through linear and spherical lenses of different shapes and orientations, before landing on a detector. The extension task was to produce a function that would optimise the number of rays falling within a specified radius on the detecter, by changing only the radii of curvature of the spherical lenses. This project was arguably the largest academic challenge I had to overcome in my degree. Training in programming was very limitted and entire concepts like functions, loops, modules and objects were either unknown or a complete blur at first. However, I rose to the challenge and revelled in the opportunity to learn new things. I am very proud of the result considering the circumstances.
